About Me

Mohaddeseh Esmaeili Farsani earned her B.C. degree in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Isfahan and is currently pursuing her Master’s at the same university.

Her research interests span data mining, machine learning, deep learning, digital health, and more recently, neuroscience and brain functionalization. She first explored this domain during her undergraduate thesis, where she analyzed EEG signals to classify individuals with Schizophrenia and healthy controls. This experience sparked a lasting interest in understanding brain function through computational methods.

Currently, she is working on recommendation systems for COVID-19 drug therapies in healthcare. Her work integrates cutting-edge technologies in health informatics, leveraging machine learning and statistical methods to enhance clinical decision-making and support personalized treatment approaches.

Research Interests

  • EEG analysis; neuroscience & brain functionalization
  • Digital Health & Clinical Decision Support
  • Data Mining, Feature Engineering, and Predictive Modeling

Current Projects

EEG-based Brain Functionalization: Applying deep learning to EEG signals to sleep classification by Dreem headband dataset.
